## Template Usage Notes

**For Authors**:
- Not every section is mandatory for every topic
- Adapt structure to fit the subject matter
- Prioritize clarity over completeness
- Cite sources inline where possible
- Link liberally to related documents
- Use plain language; explain jargon

**Tone Guidelines**:
- **Balanced**: Show multiple perspectives
- **Evidence-based**: Cite data, historical records, primary sources
- **Critical**: Question assumptions, show limitations
- **Accessible**: Write for intelligent layperson, not just economists
- **Honest**: Acknowledge uncertainty, unresolved debates

**Visual Elements**:
- Use Mermaid diagrams for: timelines, flowcharts, concept maps, relationships
- Use tables for: comparisons, data, summaries
- Use blockquotes for: important definitions, key quotes

**Cross-Referencing**:
- Always link to related documents when mentioned
- Use relative paths: `../crises/great-depression.md`
- Link both ways (from A to B, and B to A)

**Quality Standards**:
- Minimum 1500 words for major topics
- At least 5 primary/secondary sources cited
- Minimum 5 cross-references to other documents
- At least one concrete example or case study
- Present at least two different schools' perspectives on contested topics
