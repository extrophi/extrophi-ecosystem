# Key Figures in Economic History

> The thinkers, practitioners, and mysterious inventors who shaped economic thought

Economics is driven by ideas, and ideas come from people. This directory profiles the major figures whose work has defined how we understand markets, money, and human behavior.

## Classical Era Founders

### [Adam Smith (1723-1790)](adam-smith.md)
**Status**: üìù Outline needed

**Magnum Opus**: *An Inquiry into the Nature and Causes of the Wealth of Nations* (1776)

**Key Ideas**:
- **Invisible hand**: Self-interest ‚Üí social benefit in competitive markets
- **Division of labor**: Specialization increases productivity (pin factory example)
- **Free trade** vs. mercantilist protectionism
- **Labor theory of value** (early version)

**Often Misunderstood**:
- Not purely laissez-faire; supported public goods, education, infrastructure
- *Theory of Moral Sentiments* (1759) emphasizes empathy, social bonds
- Critical of corporations (joint-stock companies) for agency problems

**Influence**: Father of classical economics; cited by both free-market advocates and critics

**Cross-references**: [Classical Economics](../schools-of-thought/classical.md), [Mercantilism](../political-economy/mercantilism.md)

### [David Ricardo (1772-1823)](ricardo.md)
**Status**: üìù Outline needed

**Magnum Opus**: *On the Principles of Political Economy and Taxation* (1817)

**Key Ideas**:
- **Comparative advantage**: Trade benefits both parties even if one is better at everything
- **Iron law of wages**: Wages tend toward subsistence level (pessimistic)
- **Ricardian equivalence**: Government debt = future taxes (rational expectations precursor)
- **Rent theory**: Landowners gain from scarcity

**Background**: Stockbroker who became economist; made fortune, then entered politics

**Influence**: Shaped free trade policy (Corn Laws repeal); influenced Marx's labor theory

**Cross-references**: [Comparative Advantage](../foundations/comparative-advantage.md), [Classical Economics](../schools-of-thought/classical.md)

### [Karl Marx (1818-1883)](karl-marx.md)
**Status**: üìù Outline needed

**Magnum Opus**: *Das Kapital* (Vol. 1: 1867, Vols. 2-3 posthumous)

**Key Ideas**:
- **Labor theory of value**: Workers create all value; capitalists extract surplus
- **Class struggle**: History driven by conflict between exploiters and exploited
- **Alienation**: Workers separated from products of their labor
- **Tendency of profit rate to fall**: Competition ‚Üí mechanization ‚Üí crises
- **Historical materialism**: Economic base determines political/cultural superstructure

**Predictions**:
- ‚úÖ Increasing concentration of capital (monopolies)
- ‚úÖ Globalization of production
- ‚úÖ Financial crises inherent to capitalism
- ‚ùå Immiseration of working class (wages rose in developed world)
- ‚ùå Socialist revolution in advanced capitalist countries (happened in agrarian Russia/China)

**Legacy**:
- Inspired socialist movements, communist states
- Influenced institutional, ecological, feminist economics
- Academic Marxism continues in sociology, cultural studies

**Cross-references**: [Marxian Economics](../schools-of-thought/marxian.md), [Institutional Economics](../political-economy/institutional.md)

## The Keynesians

### [John Maynard Keynes (1883-1946)](keynes.md)
**Status**: üìù Outline needed

**Magnum Opus**: *The General Theory of Employment, Interest and Money* (1936)

**Key Ideas**:
- **Aggregate demand** determines output (not Say's Law)
- **Animal spirits**: Psychological factors drive investment
- **Liquidity preference**: Money demand theory
- **Paradox of thrift**: Individual saving ‚Üí collective harm in recession
- **Fiscal policy**: Government spending to manage demand

**Background**:
- Cambridge economist, Treasury advisor
- Versailles Treaty critic (*Economic Consequences of the Peace*, 1919)
- Bretton Woods architect (1944)

**Famous Quote**: "In the long run we are all dead" (critique of classical patience)

**Legacy**: Dominated 1936-1970s; New Keynesian revival post-2008

**Cross-references**: [Keynesian Economics](../schools-of-thought/keynesian.md), [Great Depression](../crises/great-depression.md), [Bretton Woods](../central-banking/bretton-woods.md)

## The Austrian Champions

### [Friedrich Hayek (1899-1992)](hayek.md)
**Status**: üìù Outline needed

**Key Works**:
- *The Road to Serfdom* (1944): Central planning ‚Üí totalitarianism
- *The Use of Knowledge in Society* (1945): Knowledge problem
- *Denationalisation of Money* (1976): Competing currencies

**Key Ideas**:
- **Knowledge problem**: Central planners can't access dispersed, tacit knowledge
- **Spontaneous order**: Markets coordinate without central direction
- **Austrian Business Cycle Theory**: Credit expansion ‚Üí malinvestment
- **Catallaxy**: Market as discovery process, not equilibrium

**Keynes-Hayek Debate**: 1930s clash over stimulus vs. liquidation

**Nobel Prize**: 1974 (shared with Gunnar Myrdal)

**Influence**: Thatcher, Reagan; Bitcoin philosophy; libertarianism

**Cross-references**: [Austrian School](../schools-of-thought/austrian.md), [Bitcoin](../crypto-future/bitcoin.md), [Business Cycle](../foundations/business-cycle.md)

## The Chicago School

### [Milton Friedman (1912-2006)](friedman.md)
**Status**: üìù Outline needed

**Key Works**:
- *A Monetary History of the United States* (1963, with Anna Schwartz): Fed caused Great Depression
- *Capitalism and Freedom* (1962): Free market advocacy
- *Free to Choose* (1980): PBS series, popular economics

**Key Ideas**:
- **Monetarism**: "Inflation is always and everywhere a monetary phenomenon"
- **Permanent income hypothesis**: Consumption based on long-term income, not current
- **Natural rate of unemployment**: Can't be reduced by inflation
- **Negative income tax**: Replace welfare with direct payments

**Policy Influence**: Volcker's inflation fight, deregulation, school vouchers

**Nobel Prize**: 1976

**Cross-references**: [Chicago School](../schools-of-thought/chicago.md), [Stagflation](../crises/stagflation.md)

## Post-Keynesians & Heterodox

### [Hyman Minsky (1919-1996)](minsky.md)
**Status**: üìù Outline needed

**Key Work**: *Stabilizing an Unstable Economy* (1986)

**Key Idea**: **Financial Instability Hypothesis**
- Stability breeds instability
- Three finance stages: Hedge (safe) ‚Üí Speculative (rollover) ‚Üí Ponzi (asset appreciation needed)
- Capitalism inherently prone to debt-deflation crises

**Ignored Until**: 2008 crisis perfectly matched his model ‚Üí "Minsky moment"

**Influence**: Post-Keynesian economics, MMT, financial regulation debates

**Cross-references**: [Post-Keynesian Economics](../political-economy/post-keynesian.md), [Global Financial Crisis](../crises/gfc-2008.md)

## Behavioral Economics

### [Daniel Kahneman (1934-2024)](kahneman.md)
**Status**: üìù Outline needed

**Key Work**: *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (2011)

**Collaboration**: Amos Tversky (1937-1996) - cognitive psychology partnership

**Key Ideas**:
- **System 1 vs. System 2**: Fast intuition vs. slow reasoning
- **Prospect theory**: Loss aversion, reference dependence
- **Heuristics and biases**: Anchoring, availability, representativeness
- **Framing effects**: How questions are asked changes answers

**Nobel Prize**: 2002 (first psychologist to win economics Nobel)

**Impact**: Challenged rational agent assumption; influenced finance, policy design ("nudges")

**Cross-references**: [Behavioral Economics](../schools-of-thought/behavioral.md), [Efficient Market Hypothesis](../modern-finance/emh.md) (critique)
