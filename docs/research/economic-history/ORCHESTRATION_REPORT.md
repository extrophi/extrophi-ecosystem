## Balanced Perspective Framework

### Contested Topics Get Multi-View Treatment

**Example: Business Cycle Explanations**
- **Classical**: Self-correcting, Say's Law
- **Keynesian**: Aggregate demand shocks
- **Austrian**: Credit expansion malinvestment
- **Monetarist**: Monetary policy errors
- **Post-Keynesian**: Minsky's financial instability
- **Real Business Cycle**: Technology shocks

Each view presented fairly, then evidence examined.

### Philosophical Questions Highlighted
- What gives money value?
- Is economics a science or ideology?
- Can markets be truly free?
- Is infinite growth possible?
- Does capitalism require inequality?

**Approach**: Present arguments, acknowledge uncertainty, avoid false certainty

## Unique Features

### 1. Crypto Integration
Unlike traditional economic history, this knowledge base treats cryptocurrency as:
- Serious monetary experiment (not dismissed)
- Ideological response to 2008 crisis
- Continuation of debates (Austrian vs. Keynesian, gold vs. fiat)
- Parallel to CBDCs (centralized vs. decentralized visions)

### 2. Heterodox Inclusion
Political economy section gives equal weight to:
- Institutional, Ecological, Feminist, Post-Keynesian schools
- Not relegated to footnotes; treated as valid perspectives
- Critiques of mainstream blind spots

### 3. Crisis-Centric Learning
Crises are not "exceptions" but **central learning events**:
- Expose flaws in prevailing models
- Trigger paradigm shifts
- Reveal power dynamics
- Test theories against reality

### 4. Historical Depth
Starts with Mesopotamia (3000 BCE), not Adam Smith (1776):
- Shows money, credit, markets have ancient origins
- Islamic finance innovations often ignored
- Continuity and rupture across millennia

### 5. Interdisciplinary
Connects economics to:
- **Philosophy** (moral debates on usury, inequality)
- **Technology** (blockchain, algorithms)
- **Politics** (state power, colonialism)
- **Ecology** (growth limits, resource depletion)
- **Psychology** (behavioral biases)
