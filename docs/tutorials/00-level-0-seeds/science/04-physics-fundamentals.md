# Tutorial Outline: Physics Fundamentals

**Category**: Science
**Level**: 0 (Seed)
**Estimated Time**: 12-15 hours

## Learning Objectives

- Understand fundamental physics concepts and laws
- Apply physics to everyday phenomena
- Solve quantitative physics problems
- Develop physical intuition and reasoning
- Appreciate physics as description of natural world

## Section 1: Motion and Kinematics

**Concepts**:
- Position, velocity, acceleration
- Vectors and scalars
- Kinematics equations
- Free fall and projectile motion

**Skills**:
- Analyze motion mathematically
- Solve kinematics problems
- Apply equations appropriately

## Section 2: Forces and Newton's Laws

**Concepts**:
- Force as interaction
- Newton's three laws
- Free body diagrams
- Common forces (gravity, friction, tension)

**Skills**:
- Draw free body diagrams
- Apply Newton's laws
- Solve force problems

## Section 3: Energy

**Concepts**:
- Work and energy relationship
- Kinetic and potential energy
- Conservation of energy
- Power and efficiency

**Skills**:
- Calculate work and energy
- Apply conservation principles
- Analyze energy transformations

## Section 4: Momentum

**Concepts**:
- Momentum and impulse
- Conservation of momentum
- Collisions (elastic and inelastic)
- Center of mass

**Skills**:
- Calculate momentum
- Analyze collisions
- Apply conservation of momentum

## Section 5: Rotational Motion

**Concepts**:
- Angular velocity and acceleration
- Torque and rotational equilibrium
- Moment of inertia
- Angular momentum

**Skills**:
- Analyze rotational motion
- Calculate torque
- Apply to real systems

## Section 6: Electricity and Magnetism

**Concepts**:
- Electric charge and field
- Current, voltage, resistance
- Ohm's law and circuits
- Magnetic fields and forces

**Skills**:
- Analyze simple circuits
- Apply Ohm's law
- Understand electromagnets

## Section 7: Waves and Sound

**Concepts**:
- Wave properties (amplitude, frequency, wavelength)
- Wave types (transverse, longitudinal)
- Sound waves and Doppler effect
- Interference and resonance

**Skills**:
- Calculate wave properties
- Analyze wave phenomena
- Apply to sound and music

## Section 8: Light and Optics

**Concepts**:
- Electromagnetic spectrum
- Reflection and refraction
- Lenses and mirrors
- Color and vision

**Skills**:
- Apply law of reflection
- Use lens equations
- Analyze optical systems

## Capstone Project

**Physics Exploration Portfolio**

Demonstrate physics understanding through projects:

1. **Problem-Solving Portfolio** (50+ problems):
   - **Mechanics** (20): Motion, forces, energy, momentum
   - **Electricity** (10): Circuits, Ohm's law
   - **Waves** (10): Sound, light, optics
   - **Rotational** (10): Torque, angular momentum

   For each: diagram, given/find, equations, solution, units

2. **Experimental Projects** (5+ experiments):
   - **Free Fall**: Measure g (gravity)
   - **Pendulum**: Period vs length
   - **Circuits**: Ohm's law verification
   - **Optics**: Lens focal length
   - **Collisions**: Momentum conservation

   For each:
   - Hypothesis
   - Procedure
   - Data collection
   - Analysis and calculations
   - Conclusion and error analysis

3. **Physics of Everyday Life** (10+ phenomena):
   - Explain physics behind common experiences
   - Examples: why sky is blue, how microwaves work, why balls curve, etc.
   - Include diagrams and calculations
   - Make predictions and test

4. **Simulation and Modeling**:
   - Use PhET or similar simulations
   - Explore 5+ physics concepts
   - Document observations
   - Vary parameters systematically
   - Compare to theoretical predictions

5. **Design Project**:
   - **Build** physical device using physics
   - Examples: catapult, Rube Goldberg, simple motor, optical instrument
   - Document design process
   - Calculations and predictions
   - Test and iterate
   - Final performance analysis

6. **Physics Explanations** (5+ topics):
   - Explain to non-physicists
   - Use analogies and visuals
   - No jargon
   - Blog posts or videos
   - Demonstrate teaching ability

**Deliverables**:
- 50+ solved problems with work shown
- 5 experimental reports
- 10 everyday physics explanations
- Simulation exploration document
- Design project with documentation
- Teaching materials (5+ topics)
- Reflection on physics understanding

## Prerequisites

- Algebra proficiency
- Trigonometry basics helpful
- Mathematical thinking
- Scientific curiosity
- Willingness to experiment

## Next Steps

After completing this tutorial, you should be ready for:
- **AP Physics**: College-level mechanics and E&M
- **Modern Physics**: Relativity, quantum mechanics
- **Thermodynamics**: Heat, entropy, statistical mechanics
- **Astrophysics**: Cosmology and astronomy
- **Engineering Physics**: Applied physics
- **Quantum Mechanics**: Wave-particle duality, uncertainty
