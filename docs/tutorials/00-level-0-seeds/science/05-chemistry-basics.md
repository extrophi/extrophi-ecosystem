# Tutorial Outline: Chemistry Basics

**Category**: Science
**Level**: 0 (Seed)
**Estimated Time**: 10-12 hours

## Learning Objectives

- Understand fundamental chemistry concepts
- Apply atomic and molecular theory
- Balance chemical equations and perform stoichiometry
- Predict chemical behavior using periodic trends
- Conduct safe laboratory experiments

## Section 1: Matter and Atomic Structure

**Concepts**:
- States of matter and phase changes
- Atoms: protons, neutrons, electrons
- Atomic number and mass
- Isotopes and ions

**Skills**:
- Identify elements by atomic structure
- Calculate atomic mass
- Understand atomic notation

## Section 2: Periodic Table

**Concepts**:
- Periodic table organization
- Groups and periods
- Metals, nonmetals, metalloids
- Periodic trends (size, electronegativity, ionization)

**Skills**:
- Navigate periodic table
- Predict element properties
- Use periodic trends

## Section 3: Chemical Bonding

**Concepts**:
- Ionic vs covalent bonding
- Lewis structures
- Molecular geometry (VSEPR)
- Polarity and intermolecular forces

**Skills**:
- Draw Lewis structures
- Predict molecular shapes
- Determine polarity

## Section 4: Chemical Reactions

**Concepts**:
- Types of reactions (synthesis, decomposition, combustion)
- Balancing chemical equations
- Law of conservation of mass
- Energy in reactions (exothermic/endothermic)

**Skills**:
- Balance chemical equations
- Classify reaction types
- Predict products

## Section 5: Stoichiometry

**Concepts**:
- Mole concept and Avogadro's number
- Molar mass calculations
- Stoichiometric ratios
- Limiting reactants

**Skills**:
- Convert between moles, grams, molecules
- Perform stoichiometric calculations
- Identify limiting reactants

## Section 6: Solutions and Concentration

**Concepts**:
- Solutions, solvents, solutes
- Molarity and concentration
- Dilution calculations
- Solubility and saturation

**Skills**:
- Calculate molarity
- Perform dilution calculations
- Understand solution chemistry

## Section 7: Acids, Bases, and pH

**Concepts**:
- Acid and base definitions
- pH scale and calculations
- Strong vs weak acids/bases
- Neutralization reactions

**Skills**:
- Calculate pH
- Predict acid-base behavior
- Balance neutralization equations

## Section 8: Chemical Safety and Lab Skills

**Concepts**:
- Laboratory safety rules
- Proper chemical handling
- Measurement and precision
- Experimental design

**Skills**:
- Work safely in laboratory
- Use lab equipment properly
- Collect accurate data

## Capstone Project

**Chemistry Mastery Portfolio**

Comprehensive chemistry demonstrations:

1. **Problem-Solving Collection** (50+ problems):
   - **Atomic Structure** (10): Atoms, isotopes, ions
   - **Periodic Trends** (5): Predict properties
   - **Bonding** (10): Lewis structures, molecular shapes
   - **Equations** (10): Balance reactions
   - **Stoichiometry** (10): Mole conversions, limiting reactants
   - **Solutions** (5): Molarity, dilution

   Show all work with units

2. **Laboratory Experiments** (5+ experiments):

   **Safety-conscious home/school lab work**:
   - **Density Determination**: Unknown liquids/solids
   - **Acid-Base Titration**: Vinegar acidity
   - **Stoichiometry**: Baking soda and vinegar
   - **Solutions**: Concentration determination
   - **Phase Changes**: Melting/boiling points

   For each:
   - Safety considerations
   - Procedure and setup photos
   - Data tables
   - Calculations
   - Error analysis
   - Conclusions

3. **Molecular Model Building**:
   - Build 20+ molecular models (physical or digital)
   - Show Lewis structures
   - Predict geometry with VSEPR
   - Determine polarity
   - Photograph or screenshot models

4. **Periodic Table Deep Dive**:
   - Choose 10 elements across periodic table
   - Research history and discovery
   - Properties and uses
   - Atomic structure details
   - Periodic trend analysis
   - Applications in daily life

5. **Chemical Phenomenon Investigation**:
   - **Choose topic**: Combustion, corrosion, fermentation, etc.
   - Explain chemistry involved
   - Molecular-level description
   - Real-world applications
   - Demonstration or experiment
   - Safety considerations

6. **Chemistry in Daily Life** (10+ examples):
   - Cooking: Maillard reaction, caramelization
   - Cleaning: Soap chemistry, bleach
   - Health: Medication, digestion
   - Environment: Photosynthesis, pollution
   - Technology: Batteries, plastics

   For each: chemical equations and explanations

**Deliverables**:
- 50+ solved chemistry problems
- 5 experimental reports with photos
- 20+ molecular models documented
- 10-element periodic table study
- Phenomenon investigation report
- Daily life chemistry examples
- Reflection on chemistry learning

## Prerequisites

- Algebra fundamentals
- Mathematical thinking
- Safety consciousness
- Precision and attention to detail
- Scientific curiosity

## Next Steps

After completing this tutorial, you should be ready for:
- **Organic Chemistry**: Carbon compounds, functional groups
- **Biochemistry**: Biological molecules and processes
- **Physical Chemistry**: Thermodynamics, kinetics
- **Analytical Chemistry**: Quantitative analysis
- **Environmental Chemistry**: Pollution, sustainability
- **Materials Science**: Polymers, ceramics, composites
